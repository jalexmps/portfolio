Context

A critical tool with a critical rating

NOS NET is the router management app that lets customers control their home network — Wi-Fi settings, parental controls, and advanced configurations. Despite being a daily utility for hundreds of thousands of NOS customers, it had one of the lowest ratings across the entire NOS app ecosystem. Users weren't just frustrated — they were calling support instead of opening the app.

The Problem

Designed for experts. Used by everyone.

The app was built from the perspective of a network engineer, not a typical home user. Technical jargon — DNS, DMZ, port forwarding — dominated the top-level navigation, leaving everyday customers overwhelmed before they'd even started.

The homepage presented every feature at once with no hierarchy or guidance. Users feared that tapping the wrong setting might break their internet connection — so instead of engaging with the app, they called support.

"Users avoided the app — or called support instead."

Key pain points

  • ↓ Technical jargon (DNS, DMZ, port forwarding) surfaced at top level
  • ↓ Overwhelming homepage with no clear hierarchy or priority
  • ↓ No guidance or error recovery — users were left to figure it out alone
  • ↓ Users feared breaking their internet connection
  • ↓ Support calls used as a substitute for self-service

Hidden Challenge

The real obstacle was internal

Beyond the UX problems visible in the ratings, there was a deeper challenge: internal resistance. The engineering team held a widely shared belief that the problem wasn't the app — it was the users.

The Belief

"The problem isn't the app — it's the users." This assumption was deeply embedded and blocked progress at every turn. If users were the problem, there was nothing to fix.

The Barrier

Without team alignment, even a well-designed solution would face resistance at every stage — from prioritisation to implementation to release. The design work alone wasn't enough.

My Approach

I used research — not opinion — to challenge the assumption. Data and direct user testimony made the real problem undeniable, and shifted the team's energy toward solving it together.

Key Insights

What research revealed

01

Users came with clear intent

Most users had one specific goal — change the Wi-Fi password or fix a connection issue. The app's structure buried these common tasks under layers of technical options they didn't understand and didn't need.

02

Fear blocked engagement

Users worried about breaking their network. When the interface looked technical and unforgiving, they disengaged rather than risk making things worse. Reducing fear was as important as reducing friction.

03

Advanced features had value — but the wrong entry point

Power users genuinely wanted access to DNS, DMZ, and port forwarding — but they were a minority. Surfacing these at the top level served the few while overwhelming the many.

Design Strategy

From control panel to guided self-service

One core shift defined the redesign: move from a technical control panel designed for experts to a guided self-service tool designed for everyone.

I

Simplify language

Replace technical terminology with plain, human language. If a user needs to know what DNS is to use the app, the app has failed — not the user.

II

Prioritise common tasks

Surface what the majority of users actually need — Wi-Fi settings and connection troubleshooting — at the top level, immediately accessible without digging.

III

Hide complexity

Move advanced features behind an "Advanced" section. They remain accessible for power users — but stop dominating an interface built for everyone.

IV

Guide, don't expose

Replace passive feature exposure with active guidance. Troubleshooting flows, contextual hints, and confirmation patterns give users confidence at every step.

The Solution

Light, structured, approachable

A complete redesign of the app — validated through usability testing and delivered across iOS, Android, and Web via a single Flutter codebase.

Plain language

Every label, instruction, and error message rewritten in language a non-technical user can understand and act on without a second thought.

Validated journeys

Core user flows — changing Wi-Fi settings, troubleshooting connectivity — tested and refined until they required no prior network knowledge to complete.

Guided troubleshooting

Step-by-step flows that walk users through common problems — reducing fear, reducing errors, and eliminating unnecessary support calls.

Cross-platform delivery

App and Web shipped from a single Flutter codebase — a consistent experience across every platform with no divergence in logic or language.

Reflection

What this project taught me

Research can shift deeply held assumptions

The biggest design challenge wasn't the UI — it was the team's belief that users were the problem. Data and direct user testimony changed the conversation and made alignment possible. Research isn't just about insights — it's a tool for organisational change.

Simplicity builds confidence, not just usability

Users didn't just find the redesigned app easier to use — they felt safer using it. Simplifying language and structure removed the fear of making a mistake. That emotional shift was as valuable as any usability improvement.

Language is a core UX tool

Renaming "DNS Settings" to "Connection Help" isn't cosmetic — it's the difference between a user engaging and a user walking away. The words we choose determine whether an interface feels approachable or hostile.
